Darley drawing of a boy on a pony with two girls (1841) [click on to enlarge]
Consists of Darley drawings in pencil, pen-and-ink, and wash, most of which are sketches for unidentified books and magazines, though there are some illustrations for published books by Henry Wadsworth Longfellow, Washington Irving, James Fenimore Cooper, Lawrence Lovechild, and Charles Dickens. There are 14 pieces of juvenilia: the earliest is a scribble done when he was six years old; a year later, his drawing of a teddy bear already shows artistic precociousness. Included are some drawings for banknotes, which are distinguished by the rural character of such vignettes as one in the collection entitled "Bull at the haystack." His lighter vein is exemplified by such drawings as his gentle jab at the world adulation of the dancer Fanny Elssler (1810-1884) and his sketch of an armored fisherman after the style of John Flaxman. In contrast are three somber and realistic Civil War drawings.
